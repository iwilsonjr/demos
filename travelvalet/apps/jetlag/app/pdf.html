(Jet Lag Prevention Guide #023)

Traveling across time zones can cause jet lag. The severity of your jet lag depends on a variety of factors. Here’s a personalized guide to minimizing jet lag on your trip.

Your Answers:

  • Travel Direction: East
  • Time Difference: 3-6 Hours
  • Arrival Time: Morning
  • Travel Type: Leisure
  • Sleep Ease: Easy

Your Risk of Jet Lag: Mild - Moderate

When you’re headed east, jet lag tends to be more severe. That’s because it shortens your day. You need to get to sleep earlier and wake up earlier.

  • Set your watch to the time at your destination
  • Try to eat and sleep on your destinations schedule
  • Use noise-cancelling headphones
  • Consider wearing dark sunglasses
  • Keep yourself well-hydrated
  • Avoid excess alcohol on the flight

  • Stay awake until at least 8:00 pm local time
  • Do NOT take a nap
  • Stay active and alert during your first day

  • Seek out light in the morning and afternoon (beach, city sights, museums, markets, etc.)
  • Avoid activities in darker environments (theatre matinees, spelunking) for the first few days

Following these tips will reduce the effects of jet lag for a more enjoyable trip. When your trip is over, go back to the Jet Lag Jettison App for a new customized guide with tips for your trip home.
